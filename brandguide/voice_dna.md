## Who is speaking

An early-career IT/AI professional in Japan and a marathon-training runner who **documents reality with intent**. Not a coach, not an influencer, not a struggle-story protagonist. He is in a **momentum season**: grateful for the steps taken, impatient to move faster, and honest about both.

The single most important correction from v1: **do not manufacture crisis.** His life is going well and he says so. The tension in his content is not "I failed and learned" — it is *pace* ("I wish I moved faster, but each step at a time"), *comparison* (the trap he names and sidesteps), and *fine lines* (training hard without injury, investing in ideas without ego). Forced vulnerability reads instantly fake on him and is why the old drafts never got shot.

## The emotional baseline

- **Gratitude without cliché:** "I am very grateful for having taken the steps needed to get to where I am right now." Plain, specific, no journey-speak.
- **Earned confidence, listed flatly:** "I can do the thing I was super afraid of, I can pick up things fast, learn, talk with stakeholders, manage projects, etc." He proves capability by listing it, not performing it.
- **Proof-by-action over claims:** "I even built a personal software to keep track of my training. That's how I connect the dots of my areas of interest"
- **Regret named, then released:** "I wish I had taken better decisions, but these are things that already has passed."
- **Control-focus stoicism:** "These are all things not under my control — or if they are, I am already doing my best."
- **Forward close:** "And the best part — I am just getting started."

## Three registers (mapped to production tiers)

### 1. RAW TALKING HEAD — the unscripted register
How he actually talks to camera with no script. Flowing sentences that stack clauses as thoughts arrive; enthusiasm allowed to repeat itself ("I am so excited… I am very excited"); concrete specifics dropped casually (30–40 km/week, Strava, HIIT, stakeholders, recruiters). Grammar imperfection is texture, not error — never polish it into corporate English.
> "A lot of things that was always just reading in books but never doing them myself."

### 2. SCRIPTED HEADSHOT — the sharpened register
His own edited piece is the reference (see the sample in `voice_dna_block.txt`). Rhetorical structure is welcome HERE and only here: a hook that rejects the expected answer, direct questions to the viewer, short punchy pivots, plain words with occasional heat ("What the hell is actually missing for me to get there?"), a systems-thinking close ("Project management 101, applies to everything."). Sharp, but never preachy — he asks the questions of *himself* first.

### 3. TEXT / LINKEDIN — the reflective diary register
His journal entries are the reference: names real people and companies (a recruiter from Robert Walters, lunch with a TekSystems recruiter), narrates decisions transparently, uses **numbered/bulleted lists when weighing options** (WFH, overtime, distance, role scope), evaluates honestly without malice, ends with a plan or a shrug ("If not, well, that's it."). Casual markers live here: "tbh", "I reckon", "idk".

## Confirmed natural markers (from HIS samples only)

- "I reckon" / "tbh" / "idk" / "well, that's it"
- Listing capabilities or criteria plainly, often as bullets
- Real names, real companies, real numbers — specificity is his credibility
- Cross-pillar bleed is natural: he builds software *for* his running, negotiates *in* Japanese — don't silo the pillars
- Self-questioning as content: "What is ego and emotional attachment, how is it causing noise?"
- "like-minded people trying to do hard things"

## Anti-voice (instant fail)

- Over-acted, over-complicated content: elaborate routines (workouts, Claude setups, study systems) presented as performance
- Anything that "enhances the idea of super performative people, which can make others feel they are doing less" — his content should make people feel *capable*, never behind
- Manufactured struggle, forced admissions, crisis arcs he isn't living
- Influencer CTAs, hype vocabulary, thought-leader posturing
- Over-corrected polished English that erases his natural texture

## Core themes (what the voice talks about)

Away from the noise · iteration and clarity over raw effort · the books-vs-doing gap · ego/attachment as noise, when to let go · fine-line balance (train hard, don't get injured — generalizes to everything) · step-at-a-time vs. wanting to move faster · earning what you're worth · doing hard things alongside like-minded people.

## Durable empirical findings (kept from the old strategy log — validated on real generations, independent of v1's rules)

1. **Source concreteness drives quality.** Rich, specific input → clean honest drafts; thin/abstract input → the model fabricates specifics. Fix inputs upstream; never ship invented details.
2. **No fabrication, ever:** no invented backstory, stats, tools, events, or quoted dialogue not present in the source idea.
3. **Voice must override any framework/template** whenever they conflict.
