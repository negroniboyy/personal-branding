
# LinkedIn — Career / Learning / Systems

## Positioning
Professional growth through real experience, not theory.  
Learning in public, but grounded, honest, and in progress.

Tone:
- Curious, not authoritative
- Clear, not performative
- Reflective, not motivational
- Honest about mistakes and gaps

---

## 1. Learning in Public (Core Pillar)

### Topics
- What I learned this week (Linux, Docker, networking, etc.)
- Concepts that didn’t make sense at first, and what changed
- Mistakes or misunderstandings and how I corrected them
- Practical breakdowns of tools I’m using
- How I am approaching learning (what works, what doesn’t)

### Angles
- From confusion → partial understanding
- Showing the process, not the result
- Explaining simply, as if documenting for myself
- Staying in a learner mindset

---

## 2. Real Work Experience (IT / Systems)

### Topics
- Lessons from internal IT work
- Small troubleshooting situations and what they taught me
- Moments where things didn’t go as expected
- Why documentation or communication made a difference
- What surprised me about working in IT day-to-day

### Angles
- Grounded in real situations, not theory
- Focus on what I noticed, not what I mastered
- Emphasis on observation and learning
- Avoiding “tips” tone, more like sharing experience

---

## 3. Career Direction & Standards

### Topics
- What I’m trying to figure out in my career right now
- What I’m looking for in my next role (and why)
- How my expectations are evolving
- Conversations I’m having with people in the industry
- What I still don’t know but want to explore

### Angles
- Thinking out loud, but structured
- No strong conclusions, just direction
- Honest about uncertainty
- Showing intent without sounding definitive

---

## 4. Building & Projects

### Topics
- Things I’m building (automation, scripts, systems)
- Why I started building them
- Where I got stuck and how I approached it
- Iterations and small improvements
- What I underestimated before starting

### Angles
- Process over results
- Trial and error
- Small wins and adjustments
- Learning through doing, not showcasing outcomes

---

## 5. Systems Thinking

### Topics
- How I break down problems when I don’t understand them
- Structuring learning or workflows step by step
- Turning messy situations into something clearer
- Observations about how systems work (technical or personal)

### Angles
- Clarity over complexity
- Showing thinking process, not final answers
- Avoiding “framework” tone
- Practical and grounded

---

## 6. Reflection Posts

### Topics
- What changed in how I think about work recently
- Lessons from the last few weeks
- Things that didn’t go as planned
- Balancing work, learning, and life
- Moments where I had to adjust my approach

### Angles
- Calm and reflective
- No hype or big claims
- Focus on small shifts in thinking
- Writing like a cleaned-up version of a diary entry

---

## 7. Recurring Formats

### Ideas
- “This week I learned…” (specific and grounded)
- “I didn’t understand this before, now I see it like this…”
- Short reflection after solving a small issue
- Monthly check-in (what I focused on, what changed)
- A small situation → what I noticed → what I’ll do differently

# Cross-Platform Alignment

## LinkedIn shows:

# Core Identity Across Both

- Structured but not rigid
- Curious and observant
- Consistent but not performative
- In progress, not finished
- Interested and willing to learn from others

---

# Usage

You can:
- Pick 2–3 pillars to focus on initially
- Rotate topics naturally based on your week
- Use real situations instead of forcing topics

Example:
Same week →
- Instagram: moment or experience
- LinkedIn: what you learned or noticed from it
