# Campaign 01 — "Build Like a Body" (Niche-Discovery Sprint)

**Created:** 2026-06-22 · **Status:** Ready to execute · **Owner:** Max
**Anchor:** Builder in public · **Platform:** IG Reels-first (LinkedIn = text repurpose) · **Audience:** Multidisciplinary people · **Run:** 4 weeks / 3× week (12 pieces)

> This is a *niche-discovery* campaign, not a content dump. The arc is deliberately linear so that by the end you (and your audience) can see which thread pulls hardest — code, body, or learning — and you double down on it for Campaign 02.

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Name:** *Build Like a Body* — the builder who trains the way he ships.

**One-sentence summary:** A 4-week reels-first arc that introduces Max as a builder in public whose edge is that he builds software the same way he builds his body and his languages — in public, imperfectly, on purpose.

**Primary objective (awareness + audience-fit signal):**
Publish 12 reels in 4 weeks and identify, from real engagement, which of the three pillars (Build / Body / Learn) earns the most saves + follows from multidisciplinary viewers.

**Secondary objectives:**
- Establish a recognizable voice ("not gonna lie", raw > polished) and visual identity (real broll, not stock).
- Convert the existing 7 approved + 2 ideas into a finished, shipped body of work (kills the "stuck in workshop" problem).
- Build the muscle of *shipping on cadence* — the meta-goal from your own checkpoint.

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary:** Multidisciplinary builders — people who code *and* train *and* learn a language/skill, and feel slightly scattered because no single "niche" holds them. They lurk on building-in-public IG/X, follow indie hackers, but quietly resent that those accounts are "all laptop, no life."

**Profile:**
> A self-taught maker (often a career-switcher or solo builder) struggling to reconcile many interests into one identity, looking for permission to be a generalist who ships. They discover people through honest reels and saved-for-later "process" content, and care most about *realness over polish* and *systems they can copy*.

**Pain points / motivations:** Scattered identity, perfectionism paralysis, "am I allowed to post before I'm an expert?", craving proof that a generalist path works.

**Where they are:** IG Reels (primary), then LinkedIn (the more "professional builder" cut), indie-hacker / r/SideProject style communities.

**Buying stage equivalent:** Top-of-funnel awareness. The "conversion" here is a *follow + save*, not a sale.

---

## 3. Key Messages

**Core message:** *You don't have to pick one thing — you have to ship the way you train: imperfect, in public, on a schedule.*

**Supporting messages (each = a pillar to test):**
1. **Build** — "I build in public, vibe-code past my level, break things, and fix them out loud." *(Proof: TurboBaba, AnythingLLM/VoIP debugging, automation-in-blocks.)*
2. **Body** — "I build my body with the same engineering mindset — diagnose, break into blocks, play the long game." *(Proof: half-marathon/knee rehab, plyometrics, calisthenics, Codewars = "gym for nerds.")*
3. **Learn** — "I'm honest about my level — including the interview that humbled me — because hiding it is the real failure." *(Proof: Japanese interview, hands-on learning, formality gap.)*
4. **Anti-perfectionism (the meta / why my content looks like this)** — "I'd rather tell the story raw than spend hours color-grading a 30-second clip." *(Proof: "No perfectionism" idea — the price-of-quality trade-off.)*

**Channel tone split:** Reels = raw, first-person, fast. LinkedIn = same story, slightly more reflective and "lessons-learned" framed.

## 5. The Linear Story Arc

Four weekly acts. Each Monday = a **main** scripted reel (depth), each Friday = a **main** reel, and a mid-week **style short** for personality/voice. The arc moves from *who I am* → *what I build* → *the discipline underneath* → *honesty + where this goes*.

```
WK1 IDENTITY      → "I'm a generalist who ships."        (manifesto + expectation-set)
WK2 THE BUILD     → "Here's me actually building."       (builder-in-public spine)
WK3 THE DISCIPLINE→ "I train the way I build."           (the differentiator: body/learn)
WK4 HONESTY+INVITE→ "Here's what humbled me. Come build." (depth + niche-test payoff)
```

## 8. Success Metrics

Primary KPI: **Pillar-fit signal** — by end of Week 4, rank Build / Body / Learn by total *saves + follows-per-view*. The winner anchors Campaign 02.

| Metric | Target (4 wks) | How tracked | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reels shipped | **12 / 12** | Studio "SHIPPED" count | Weekly |
| Saves per reel | Trend ↑ week over week | IG insights | Weekly |
| Follows from reels | Net positive, accelerating | IG insights | Weekly |
| Avg watch / retention | Identify which *format* (headshot vs. videogame vs. short) holds longest | IG insights | Weekly |
| Stories poll votes | ≥ enough to break a tie on pillar | Stories | Weekly |

Note: don't optimize for views in a niche-discovery sprint. **Saves + follows + retention** tell you who's actually your person. A 400-view reel with 30 saves beats a 4,000-view reel with 5.

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

1. **Perfectionism stalls shipping (your documented #1 risk).** → Mitigation: P1 and P2 are *literally about this*; publishing them is a commitment device. Hard rule: a reel ships at "good enough," fixes happen in the *next* reel, never by delaying the current one.
2. **Anchor/audience mismatch (build-in-public usually pulls devs, not generalists).** → Mitigation: every "build" piece is deliberately paired with a body/learn frame (P4 = code×gym, P7/P9 = the parallel grind). If Week 2 (pure build) underperforms vs. Week 3 (discipline), that *is* the niche signal — follow it.
3. **Production-heavy pieces (P4 videogame edit) blow the cadence.** → Mitigation: start P4 in Week 1; if it's not ready, swap in a style short and slide P4 to its next slot. The arc survives a one-slot shift.
