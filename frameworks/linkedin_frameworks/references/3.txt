I used to think coming up with a business idea was like waiting for a lightbulb moment. 

In reality it's more of a process, and a lot of people skip the first step.

The mistake I see almost everyone make is starting with the product before thinking about who you're actually building for. In other words, putting the product before the person.

I've done it myself with various things in the past and having an idea for a product is not wrong exactly, but the danger is that you end up building something based on your own intuition and then try to find people who want it. And that's not easy because it might not be as many people as you think.

Instead, flip it and start with a Person and a Problem. So figure out a specific type of person or avatar and a specific problem that they have. This combination of Person and Problem is your 'niche'.

And once you've got that, figuring out the product part becomes a lot more obvious. Essentially, focus in on who you serve and what pains them.

Two questions to ask yourself:

👉 Who do I actually want to help?
👉 What's genuinely frustrating them right now?

Start there. The product comes after.
