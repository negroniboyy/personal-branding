Not taking things personally is a career (and life) cheat code.

5 mental models that build quiet resilience:

1. Spotlight Effect: Nobody's watching you as closely as you think. Your freedom starts the moment you accept that.

2. Procedure or Perception Razor: When you receive criticism, ask, “Should I change what I do or my interpretation of the feedback?"

3. The 10% Hater Rule: A fixed percentage of people will dislike anything you do. It's statistics, not a personal flaw.

4. Growth Mindset: Every master was once a beginner, and mastery starts the day you commit to learning forever.

5. No Is Normal: Most of the founders you admire received hundreds of nos before a single yes. Rejection is a prerequisite to success. 

I think about a scene from The Fountainhead often.

The dean tells Howard Roark his innovative designs will never get built. "Who will let you build like that?" he asks.

Roark answers: "Who will stop me?"

Never let the person stopping you be you.
