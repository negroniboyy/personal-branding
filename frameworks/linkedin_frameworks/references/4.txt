Being "almost ready" to start a business almost always means you're stuck on one of three things. 

And the trick is figuring out which one, because the fix is different for each.

1. The idea. You don't have one yet. Or you've got fifteen and can't pick. You keep refreshing your Notes app hoping the right one will surface. (It won't, btw. Ideas don't get clearer by staring at them, they get clearer by being filtered through a process.)

2. The niche. You've got an idea but it's something like "I want to help people with productivity" and you can feel, in your gut, that it's too vague to actually sell. You can't finish the sentence "I help [specific person] do [specific thing]." So nothing moves.

3. The offer. You know what you want to do, you know who it's for, but you've got no idea what to actually charge, or how to ask the first person to pay. So you keep "preparing." For months. Years, in some cases.

Honestly, just naming which one you're stuck on is half the work. Most people think they're stuck on all three at once. They're not. They're stuck on one, and the other two get easier once that one moves.
