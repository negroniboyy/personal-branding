If you’re job hunting: the job title matters less than who you report to.

“Your first boss is the biggest factor in your career success. A boss who doesn’t trust you won’t give you opportunities to grow.”

Every leader thinks they’re the trustworthy one. Most aren’t.

The difference between someone thriving and someone languishing at the same company often isn’t the role, it’s the manager. 

Whether they’ll actually delegate. 
Whether they’ll advocate for you when you’re not in the room. 
Whether they see your potential or just your output.

For leaders: Before you onboard your next hire, ask yourself what opportunities you’ve actually created for people.

For job seekers: Don’t negotiate the job. Negotiate who you report to. 
Ask about their last three reports. 
Where are they now? 
Would they work for this person again?

Your career success depends in that relationship.
